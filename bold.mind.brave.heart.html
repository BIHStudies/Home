Course At a Glance

Purpose: To equip thoughtful Bhakti practitioners with the philosophical grounding and communication skills needed to speak clearly, confidently, and responsibly in modern scientific and intellectual settings.

Bold Minds, Brave Hearts is a university-style training series offered across two academic terms. Together we will explore core topics in consciousness, metaphysics, cosmology, and ethics, and learn how to express these ideas with clarity and integrity.

Faculty & Curriculum Overview

Guided by scholars with deep expertise in metaphysics, cosmology, and consciousness studies.

Unit 1: Jan 10 – Jan 31

Foundations of Metaphysics

Dr. Howard Resnick

  • Introduction to metaphysics: arguments against atheism and physicalism
  • Metaphysics in history: Plato’s principles; Aristotle’s parallels to Caitanya’s logic
  • Case study of irrational materialism & false Darwinist claims
  • Physicalism vs. civilization: how strict materialism denies moral principles
Unit 2: Feb 7 – Feb 28

Philosophy Foundations

Dr. Shivanand Sharma

  • Argumentation: logical reasoning and debate methodology
  • Philosophy of mind: consciousness and cognitive frameworks
  • Philosophy of science: scientific methodology and its limitations
  • Philosophy of religion: theological and spiritual epistemology
Unit 3: March 14 – April 4

Physics and Cosmology

Dr. Doug Watson

  • Crisis in physics: modern physics’ conceptual limitations
  • Rethinking space, time, and matter: quantum mechanics challenging fixed assumptions
  • Quantum gravity: emerging process-based understandings of reality
  • Science and philosophy: relational cosmology integrating consciousness
Unit 4: April 11 – May 2

Vedic Cosmology

Dr. Tiziano Valentinuzzi

  • Crisis of meaning: how physicalism fails to satisfy human purpose
  • Consciousness-based universe: ātmā-centered worldview vs. neuroscience
  • Simulation hypothesis: Vedic metaphysics through quantum physics
  • Divine love scientifically: bhakti as a rational principle
Unit 5: May 9 – June 27

Consciousness Studies

Akhandadhi Das

  • Consciousness: understanding consciousness; the “Hard Problem”
  • Reality: metaphysical foundations and consciousness–world interaction
  • Life: Earth’s biological mysteries; scientific support for Purāṇic perspectives
  • Cosmos: consciousness-based universal structure and realms of free consciousness

Certification & Assessment

To support meaningful learning, each Unit concludes with a short reflective or practical assessment. A Unit Certificate is awarded upon completion of each Unit. Students who complete all five Units receive the Final Program Certificate, recognizing successful completion of the full six-month training.

Learning Outcomes & Enrollment Options

The learning outcomes include:

  • Clearer understanding of metaphysics, cosmology, consciousness, and ethics
  • Improved reasoning, articulation, and communication skills
  • Increased confidence engaging in scientific, philosophical, or interfaith conversations
  • Deeper appreciation of the Bhagavata worldview and its contemporary relevance

A) Full Program Enrollment — $108

This single program fee provides access to the entire six-month training, including all live Saturday sessions, Canvas modules, assignments, and assessments. Students also receive a Unit Certificate for each completed Unit and a Final Program Certificate upon successful completion of all requirements. This option offers the greatest value, supporting a coherent journey that builds progressively from foundational metaphysics to applied consciousness studies. Students may participate synchronously or complete the material asynchronously through Canvas, depending on their schedule and learning needs.

B) Per-Unit Enrollment — $30 per Unit

For those unable to commit to the full program at this time, individual Units may be taken separately for $30 each. Per-unit participation includes access to the Canvas environment, recordings for the selected Unit, and a Unit Certificate upon completion of its assessment.
