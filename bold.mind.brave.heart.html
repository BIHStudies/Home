Bold Minds, Brave Hearts

Communicating Timeless Wisdom in a Scientific Age

A 6-Month Training Series by the Bhaktivedanta Institute for Higher Studies

Purpose

To equip thoughtful Bhakti practitioners with the philosophical grounding and communication skills needed to speak confidently and responsibly in modern scientific and intellectual settings.

Description

This 6-month training series is designed for educators, scholars, and thoughtful Bhakti practitioners who want to engage deeply with contemporary scientific and philosophical questions. Participants will explore core ideas in consciousness, metaphysics, cosmology, and ethics, and learn how to express these concepts with clarity and confidence in modern intellectual settings.

Through study, dialogue, and guided skill-building, the course offers a reasoned and experiential approach to Bhagavata philosophy — one that encourages reflection, critical inquiry, meaningful communication, and practical application.

Educational Philosophy

Bold Minds, Brave Hearts is designed for students who wish to explore and communicate the philosophical foundations of the Bhāgavata tradition in contemporary scientific and intellectual contexts. Our approach emphasizes clarity, intellectual honesty, and thoughtful dialogue. Students are encouraged to examine Bhāgavata perspectives through reasoned inquiry and to express them in ways that respectfully engage modern scientific and philosophical thought.

The course is guided by scholars with expertise in metaphysics, cosmology, consciousness studies, ethics, and the history of ideas — along with a deep, lived grounding in the Bhāgavata tradition. This combination offers participants both intellectual depth and authentic insight.

Faculty & Curriculum Overview

Unit 1: Foundations of Metaphysics (4 Sessions – Spring Semester Starts January 10)

Faculty: Dr. Howard Resnick

  • Introduction to metaphysics: arguments against atheism, physicalism, and blind evolution
  • Metaphysics in history: Plato’s Kṛṣṇa conscious principles; Aristotle’s parallels to Caitanya’s logic; and more
  • Case study of irrational materialism: how 18th–19th century conditions led to false Darwinist claims
  • Physicalism vs. civilization: how strict materialism denies moral principles and human values

Unit 2: Philosophy Foundations (4 Sessions – Spring Semester)

Faculty: Dr. Shivanand Sharma

  • Argumentation: logical reasoning and debate methodology
  • Philosophy of mind: consciousness and cognitive frameworks
  • Philosophy of science: scientific methodology and its limitations
  • Philosophy of religion: theological and spiritual epistemology

Unit 3: Physics and Cosmology (4 Sessions – Winter Semester Starts March 14)

Faculty: Dr. Doug Watson

  • Crisis in physics: modern physics’ conceptual limitations and philosophical blind spots
  • Rethinking space, time, and matter: quantum mechanics challenging fixed assumptions
  • Quantum gravity: emerging process-based understandings of reality
  • Science and philosophy: relational cosmology integrating consciousness and meaning

Unit 4: Vedic Cosmology (4 Sessions – Winter Semester)

Faculty: Dr. Tiziano Valentinuzzi

  • Crisis of meaning: how physicalism fails to satisfy human purpose
  • Consciousness-based universe: ātmā-centered worldview vs. neuroscience and panpsychism
  • Simulation hypothesis: Vedic metaphysics through quantum physics and information theory
  • Divine love scientifically: bhakti as a rational principle rather than sentiment

Unit 5: Consciousness Studies (8 Sessions – Winter Semester)

Faculty: Akhandadhi Das

  • Consciousness: understanding consciousness; the “Hard Problem” and physicalist failures
  • Reality: metaphysical foundations and consciousness–world interaction
  • Life: Earth’s biological mysteries; scientific support for Purāṇic perspectives
  • Cosmos: consciousness-based universal structure and realms of free consciousness

Implementation & Program Contribution

Certification & Assessment

To support meaningful learning, each Unit concludes with a short reflective or practical assessment. A Unit Certificate is awarded upon completion of each Unit. Students who complete all five Units receive the Final Program Certificate, recognizing successful completion of the full six-month training.

Program Contribution & Enrollment Options

Bold Minds, Brave Hearts is intended as a six-month guided training, and the full program offers the richest learning experience. Students who enroll in the complete course gain access to all five Units, the full Canvas learning environment, recordings of each session, course materials, and both Unit Certificates and the Final Program Certificate upon completion. To make the training accessible to a wide range of sincere learners, BIHS offers two ways to participate:

A) Full Program Enrollment — $108

This single program fee provides access to the entire six-month training, including all live Saturday sessions, Canvas modules, assignments, and assessments. Students also receive a Unit Certificate for each completed Unit and a Final Program Certificate upon successful completion of all requirements. This option offers the greatest value, supporting a coherent journey that builds progressively from foundational metaphysics to applied consciousness studies. Students may participate synchronously or complete the material asynchronously through Canvas, depending on their schedule and learning needs.

B) Per-Unit Enrollment — $30 per Unit

Scholarship Support

For those unable to commit to the full program at this time, individual Units may be taken separately for $30 each. Per-unit participation includes access to the Canvas environment, recordings for the selected Unit, and a Unit Certificate upon completion of its assessment.

Time Investment & Expected Gains

The training meets once per week on Saturday for two hours, over two three-month period. Participants can expect to devote an additional 1–2 hours per week for reading, reflection, or short assignments.

The learning outcomes include:

  • Clearer understanding of metaphysics, cosmology, consciousness, and ethics
  • Improved reasoning, articulation, and communication skills
  • Increased confidence engaging in scientific, philosophical, or interfaith conversations
  • Deeper appreciation of the Bhagavata worldview and its contemporary relevance
